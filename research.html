2025

Success and Failure of a Zero-Interest Green Loan Program: Evidence from France

Forthcoming, Journal of the Association of Environmental and Resource Economists (JAERE)
with Ilya Eryzhenskiy and Louis-Gaëtan Giraudet

We provide the first evaluation of a Zero-Interest Green Loan (ZIGL) program for home energy retrofits. Applying an event-study design to a program introduced in France in 2009, we find that eligibility increased retrofit investment by 24% on the extensive margin and 4% on the intensive margin, and induced 1.5% of eligible households to switch away from fossil-fuel heating. Effects become non-significant after two years and are primarily driven by low-income homeowners, suggesting the program alleviated credit constraints.

2023

Effectiveness and supply effects of high-coverage rent control policies

Regional Science and Urban Economics, 2023
with Jordi Jofre-Monseny and Rodrigo Martínez Mazza

We study a rent control policy adopted in Catalonia in September 2020 that covers virtually the entire rental market. Using difference-in-differences and event-study designs, we find the regulation reduced average rents paid by about 6%, with no reduction in the supply of housing units in the rental market.

The impact of taxing vacancy on housing markets: Evidence from France

Journal of Public Economics, 2020

The first evaluation of a tax on vacant housing. I develop a model of vacancy creation, then use the quasi-experimental implementation of a French vacancy tax in 1999 with an exhaustive fiscal dataset covering every dwelling in France from 1995–2013. A difference-in-differences approach combined with propensity score matching suggests the tax reduced vacancy rates by 13% between 1997 and 2001, concentrated in long-term vacancy.

2020

Do short-term rental platforms affect rents and prices? Evidence from Airbnb in Barcelona

Journal of Urban Economics, 2020
with Miquel-Àngel Garcia-López, Jordi Jofre-Monseny and Rodrigo Martínez Mazza

We assess the impact of Airbnb's entry on housing rents and prices in Barcelona. Several estimation approaches exploiting the geography and timing of Airbnb's entry indicate that neighbourhoods with higher Airbnb penetration experienced increased prices and rents, with the price effect larger than the rent effect.

Working papers

2025

The economics of cycling: how a large-scale bike network reshapes local demand

Working paper, 2025 — R&R at Journal of Economic Geography
with Federica Daniele, Pablo Warnes, Youssouf Camara and David Bounie

Previously circulated as "Bike-friendly cities: an opportunity for local businesses? Evidence from the city of Paris." We study the effects on brick-and-mortar consumption of Paris's large-scale cycling infrastructure (Plan Vélo) using geolocated card-transaction data and variation in market access from the new network. Areas with improved access saw a quarterly consumption rise of 4.4%, with larger gains for smaller and younger establishments, alongside a decline in car traffic and no evidence of impact on business entry or house prices.

2025

What Works in Housing Policies? A Review of the Academic Literature

SSRN working paper, 2025 — submitted
with Jordi Jofre-Monseny

A systematic but non-exhaustive review of the empirical literature on housing policies such as land-use regulation, public rental housing, rent control and help-to-buy programs. Our main contribution is a four-category classification of housing policies according to their primary objectives.

2025

Golden Visas and Real Estate Markets

SSRN working paper, 2025 — new
with Clara Martínez-Toledano, Dirk Foremny and Zhengming Li

We study the impact of investor citizenship and residence schemes — "golden visa" programmes — on local real estate markets, examining Spain's programme introduced in 2013 for foreign investors investing at least €500,000. Using the universe of Spanish real estate transactions, we find non-EU transactions above the threshold rose 43% more than EU transactions post-introduction, a premium of €11,521 for non-EU investors around the threshold, and spillover effects raising overall transaction prices by 0.19% on average.

2025

When Leases Get Longer: Tenure Security in Rental Markets

First draft, 2025
with Alessandro Ciardo, Jordi Jofre-Monseny and Rodrigo Martínez Mazza

We study the impact of a 2019 Spanish reform extending minimum rental contracts from three to five years (seven for corporate landlords), using the universe of Catalan rental agreements (2016–2024) and a regression discontinuity design. Contract length rose sharply for both landlord types, with no significant effects on rents, contract volume, or short-term contracts for individual landlords. Longer contracts increase tenant stability, driven by fewer renewals and lower market exit among corporate landlords.
