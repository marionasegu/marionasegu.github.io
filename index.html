I am joining Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne as a Professor of Economics starting September. Until then, I am an Assistant Professor at THEMA and CY Cergy Paris Université, where I have been since September 2021. I am also an IEB Research Affiliate.

Previously, I was a post-doctoral researcher at INED, working on the ANR project WORKLIFE. I obtained a PhD in Economics from Université Paris-Saclay and RITM in 2020, for which I was awarded the AFSE PhD Prize.

My research fields are urban economics, housing economics, public policy and environmental economics. I study housing markets, the structure and development of cities, and the role of public authorities within them — including the tax on vacant apartments, Airbnb's entry into housing markets, rent control, and zero-interest green loans. Ongoing projects examine bike-lane networks, tenancy contract length regulation, and golden visa programs.
